Tips & Tricks for Better Prompts

Be Specific, Not Vague

Instead of "cinematic," specify exactly what makes it cinematic: the lens, lighting, camera movement, color grade. "Cinematic" alone means nothing to the AI.

Clarity

Front-Load Important Details

Put your most important elements first. AI models weight the beginning of prompts more heavily. Subject and action should come before style descriptors.

Priority

Use Real Camera References

Reference real cameras (ARRI, RED, Sony Venice) and lenses (35mm, 50mm, 85mm) for authentic film looks. The AI understands these technical terms.

Authenticity

Describe the Light Source

Don't just say "good lighting." Specify: golden hour sunlight, neon signs, single candle, overcast sky, studio softbox. Light defines the mood.

Lighting

One Camera Move Per Shot

Stacking multiple complex movements creates chaos. Pick one clear movement and let it breathe. "Dolly in" is better than "dolly in while orbiting and tilting."

Simplicity

Include Emotional Keywords

Add words that describe the feeling: melancholic, triumphant, eerie, peaceful, chaotic, intimate. These guide the AI's interpretation of every element.

Emotion

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Too Many Subjects

Prompting for multiple characters doing different things confuses the model. Focus on one clear subject and action per generation.

Contradictory Descriptors

"Dark moody lighting but also bright and cheerful" - pick one direction. Contradictions create incoherent results.

Negative Prompts Overdone

Long lists of "no this, no that" are less effective than clearly stating what you DO want. Focus on positive descriptors.

Ignoring Aspect Ratio

A vertical prompt in horizontal format loses composition. Match your prompt description to your output dimensions.

VFX & Effects

Cinematic Transitions

Studio-grade transitions with example prompts to seamlessly blend photos and videos with stunning visual effects.

Seamless Transition Smooth
Scene A transforms into Scene B through matching visual elements. Match camera movement direction, use similar colors or shapes at transition point for invisible blend.
Smoke Transition Elemental
Dense smoke billows across the frame from left to right, completely obscuring the scene. As smoke clears, new environment is revealed. Works best with moody, atmospheric content.
Flame Transition Elemental
Fire erupts from center of frame, flames licking across entire screen. Intense orange and red consume the image before burning away to reveal next scene. High energy, dramatic.
Water Bending Elemental
Liquid water flows across frame in organic waves, distorting and morphing the image. Ripples create dreamlike distortion before settling into new scene. Fluid, graceful movement.
Air Bending Elemental
Invisible wind force sweeps across frame, particles and debris carried in swirling vortex. Scene dissolves into wind-blown fragments before reforming as new location.
Splash Transition Liquid
Colorful liquid splashes against invisible surface, splattering across frame in slow motion. Paint-like droplets create abstract moment before clearing to reveal next scene.
Melt Transition Morph
Image appears to melt like wax under heat, colors dripping downward. Surreal, viscous distortion transitions to new scene emerging from the melted remains.
Plasma Explosion Energy
Bright plasma energy explodes from center, electric blue and white tendrils spreading outward. Sci-fi energy burst consumes frame before dissipating into next scene.
Northern Lights Atmospheric
Aurora borealis ribbons dance across sky, greens and purples weaving through frame. Ethereal light curtains sweep scene away, revealing new environment beneath.
Raven Transition Creature
A flock of ravens bursts across frame, black feathers filling the screen completely. Birds disperse to reveal transformed scene, dramatic and mysterious.
Glitch Transition Digital
Digital corruption glitches across frame - RGB splits, scan lines, pixel sorting. Tech malfunction aesthetic before signal clears to new, clean scene.
Ink Drop Organic
Black ink drops into water, spreading in organic tendrils. Slow-motion diffusion creates abstract patterns before ink consumes frame and clears to new scene.
Light Leak Film
Warm light leaks bleed in from frame edge, overexposing image with golden and orange flares. Vintage film artifact washes out scene before fading to reveal next.
Shatter Transition Impact
Image shatters like glass, fragments flying toward camera. Broken pieces fall away revealing new scene behind. Impactful, dramatic moment of destruction.
Particle Dissolve Sci-Fi
Subject dissolves into thousands of glowing particles, floating and swirling before reforming as new scene. Thanos-snap style disintegration effect.
Transition Technical Tips
Timing: 1-1.5 seconds for action, 2-3 seconds for emotional moments
Matching: End Scene A and start Scene B with similar colors/shapes for smoothest blends
Direction: Match movement direction (left-to-right in A, continues in B)
Max clips: 5 seconds input each, 15 seconds total output

Production Pipeline

Professional Workflow

Follow this step-by-step process to create cinematic AI videos with consistent, professional results.

1

Write Your Prompt First

Use the 6-part formula: Subject + Action + Environment + Lighting + Camera + Style. Get specific before generating.

2

Choose Your AI Model

Match the model to your need: Sora for realism, Kling for audio, WAN for camera control, Minimax for speed.

3

Upload Reference (Optional)

Start with a high-quality reference image to lock visual style, lighting, and character appearance for consistency.

4

Select Camera Movement

Pick 1-2 movements maximum. Match input type to movement (wide shots for FPV, close-ups for Snorricam).

5

Apply Visual Style

Choose film look that matches your story's mood. Consider era, genre, and emotional tone.

6

Generate & Iterate

Generate, review, refine prompt, regenerate. Each iteration teaches you what works. Save successful prompts.

7

Add Transitions

Connect scenes with appropriate transitions. Match energy levels and use visual continuity for seamless flow.

Expert Advice

Pro Tips & Best Practices

Professional techniques to elevate your AI video production from good to cinematic.

1
Match Input to Movement
For orbiting, dolly, and zooms - use a centered character or object.
For FPV drone or handheld - use a wide or scenic input so the move has space to travel.
For POV or Snorricam - use close-ups of a face or object.
For Through Object shots - be specific about the object (keyhole, doorway, window).
2
Keep Motion Simple
One clear camera move usually looks more cinematic than stacked moves. The physics engine creates realistic inertia and speed curves - let it breathe. If combining movements, limit to 2-3 and ensure they complement each other.
3
Use Reference Images
Start Frame locks the opening look for consistent style.
End Frame locks a clean finish for cuts and thumbnails.
Reference images give you control over exactly how scenes begin and end.
4
Aspect Ratio Strategy
21:9 CinemaScope - Maximum cinematic impact, film-like framing
16:9 Horizontal - YouTube, presentations, traditional video
9:16 Vertical - TikTok, Instagram Reels, Stories
1:1 Square - Instagram feed, consistent crop
5
Build a Prompt Library
Save prompts that work. Build templates for different shot types: establishing, dialogue, action, product. Iterate on winners rather than starting from scratch each time.
6
Test Multiple Models
The same prompt can produce wildly different results across models. When a shot isn't working, try a different model before rewriting the prompt. Each model has unique strengths.
