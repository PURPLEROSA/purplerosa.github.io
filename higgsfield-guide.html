Prompt Engineering

Cinematic Prompting Guide

Craft effective prompts combining clear descriptions with cinematic language for professional results.

Prompt Structure Formula

1. Main Subject

Begin with a clear, vivid description. Include clothing, appearance, mood, or character traits. Be specific about who or what is in frame.

2. Action or Movement

Describe what the subject is doing using strong action verbs. This defines the motion that happens during the camera move.

3. Camera Language

Use specific terms: "dolly in," "orbit," "handheld," "FPV." Avoid vague words like "cinematic" or "dynamic."

4. Environment Details

Describe the setting, lighting conditions, time of day, and atmosphere. This context helps the AI render the scene correctly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Vague Motion Terms

Using "cinematic" or "dynamic" doesn't tell the model how to move. The AI guesses, creating inconsistent results.

Fix: Use specific verbs like "dolly in," "orbit," "handheld"

Missing Environment

Describing only the subject without context leaves the AI to invent surroundings that may not match your vision.

Fix: Add lighting, location, time of day, atmosphere

Stacking Too Many Moves

Combining multiple complex camera movements often creates chaotic, unprofessional results.

Fix: One clear camera move usually looks more cinematic

Wrong Input for Movement

Using close-ups for FPV drone or wide shots for Snorricam creates mismatched, awkward results.

Fix: Match input type to movement requirements

Production Pipeline

Professional Workflow

Follow this step-by-step process to create cinematic AI videos with consistent, professional results.

1

Upload Your Hero Frame

Start with a high-quality reference image. This locks your visual style - lighting, color, character appearance. The model will maintain consistency from this frame.

2

Select Camera Body & Lens

Choose your virtual camera (ARRI, RED, Sony) and lens configuration. Set focal length based on your shot type - wide for environment, 50mm for portraits, 85mm for glamour.

3

Choose Camera Movement

Select 1-3 cinematic presets. Start simple - one clear move is more cinematic than stacked complexity. Match movement to input type (FPV needs wide shots, Snorricam needs close-ups).

4

Write Your Prompt

Describe the action that happens during the camera move. Camera Control locks your subject positioning while the prompt defines what happens. Use specific verbs and environment details.

5

Apply Visual Style

Choose your film look - VHS for retro, Cinematic for modern, Anamorphic for blockbuster. This sets color grading, grain, and aspect ratio.

6

Adjust Timing & Generate

Set video duration (up to 10 seconds), toggle Slow Motion for dreamy high-frame-rate look, add Start/End frames for clean cuts. Hit Generate.

7

Add Transitions & VFX

Upload clips to Transitions tool, select effects (Smoke, Flame, Seamless), and blend scenes. Stack multiple clips for longer sequences.

Expert Advice

Pro Tips & Best Practices

Professional techniques to elevate your AI video production from good to cinematic.

1
Match Input to Movement
For orbiting, dolly, and zooms - use a centered character or object.
For FPV drone or handheld - use a wide or scenic input so the move has space to travel.
For POV or Snorricam - use close-ups of a face or object.
For Through Object shots - be specific about the object (keyhole, doorway, window).
2
Keep Motion Simple
One clear camera move usually looks more cinematic than stacked moves. The physics engine creates realistic inertia and speed curves - let it breathe. If combining movements, limit to 2-3 and ensure they complement each other (e.g., Dolly In + Dutch Angle, or Crane Up + 360 Orbit).
3
Use Start/End Frames
Start Frame locks the opening look for consistent style.
End Frame locks a clean finish for cuts, thumbnails, or CTAs.
This gives you control over exactly how scenes begin and end for professional editing.
4
Leverage Presets
Presets are ready-made camera moves, styles, and templates that help generate cinematic clips without building from scratch. Benefits: faster generations, more consistent output, less prompt tweaking. Use them as starting points, then customize.
5
Transition Timing
Transitions work best at 1-1.5 seconds duration. Longer transitions can feel sluggish, shorter ones may feel jarring. For action sequences, go shorter. For emotional moments, let transitions breathe longer.
6
Layer Movements for Transitions
Use camera movements as transitions: combine FPV Drone + Super Dolly In to move between scenes smoothly. Stack effects like Glitch, Roll, or Thunder Sparks to completely transform how you move from one scene to the next.
7
Aspect Ratio Strategy
21:9 CinemaScope - Maximum cinematic impact, film-like framing
16:9 Horizontal - YouTube, presentations, traditional video
9:16 Vertical - TikTok, Instagram Reels, Stories, shorts
3:4 Portrait - Instagram feed, product showcases
8
Build Impossible Cameras
Combine effects that don't exist in reality: the grit of 16mm film with the sharpness of modern Anamorphic glass. Mix ARRI color science with Panavision lens characteristics. Create the virtual camera that serves your creative vision.
